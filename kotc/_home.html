King of the Court

How it works

Each player will play a set with every other player. Rankings at the end are determined by total wins, then point differential.

  • Location_on

    Play on the court assigned to you.

  • Leaderboard

    Games are rally scoring to 15 points, win by 2, cap at 17. Switch sides every 5 points

  • Home

    The home team chooses first serve or their preferred side

  • workspace_premium

    Prizes for today are: The friendships you make along the way.

Entering Scores

Enter scores as games are played.

  • 1

    Select a player, and tap current matchup to expand it.

  • 2

    Tap the “Enter score” button.

  • 3

    On the new scoreboard screen, tap/swipe to the correct score (you can also use this to keep score during the game if you are refing.) Once the score is correct, press the “Done” button.

  • 4

    The standings and/or playoff bracket will automatically update based on the score.

If you make an error you can go back to the matchup and press “edit score.”

If there are discrepancies in the scoring, the tournament owner can access the change logs.

Rules

We’ll be using Philly pickup rules.

For if you’re unfamiliar. some key things to remember are:

  • No let serves
  • No open hand tips/directional blocking
  • No sets of pushes over the net. A set that accidentally goes over is the other teams point.
  • Open hand receives are only allowed on hard driven attacks (meaning a continuous downward path/no arc/not off the block/no serves)
  • You are allowed to cross under the net as long as it does not interfere with the other team
  • Hair and clothing touching the net is not a fault. Hats and shoes are.
  • Most importantly, we’re here for volleyball and vibes. This isn’t the AVP tour. Don’t be a dick.

If you have more specific question, refer to East Coast Volleyball or if something is not covered there, the Pottstown Rumble Rules
