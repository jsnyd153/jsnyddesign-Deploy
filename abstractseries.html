abstract series

below are a series of photo collage experiments. they’re each composed of images taken throughout philadelphia. ispired by some of the work of martin venesky, it the experiments have a number of different rules to them: all the images are converted into single color screens, images can only be cropped at 90 degree angles (so no fancy masking, and once an image has been used in one collage it can’t appear in another collage.

black and white

the first iteration was constrained to black and white, with each featuring a single red dot. due to the cropping constraint, they rely heavily on the lines created in the original photographs to create a sense of motion and guide the eye through the composition.

the idea of incorporating a number photographed number, often from a street address, became an additional rule, and doubled as a way to name each piece.

series 1

the next iteration introduced color, and even more so having a 3rd tone, opened up a world of possibilities for the layering of the screens. while it was much easier to create powerful compositions with the classic designer toolkit of black, white and red, the bright, saturated colors provided a huge amount of additional expression to the pieces.

series 2

sereis 2 changes to a different color palette, and puts more of an emphasis on harder edges with more dramatic contrast.

series 3

series 3 takes a more dramatic tonal shift in the color palette from 1 and 2, opting for a darker purple color to ground it. the similar tonality of the yellow and bright green almost makes these two-and-a-half colors rather than the heavily contrasted three color palletes of the previous two. It puts more of an emphasis on the architectural forms, bricks, stairs, ladder and bridges, bringing it back towards the compositional elements from the original experiments.
